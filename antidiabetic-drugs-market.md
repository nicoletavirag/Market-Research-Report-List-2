<p><p>Antidiabetic drugs can be classified into two main types: oral drugs and injectable drugs. Oral drugs are medications that are taken by mouth, typically in the form of tablets or capsules. They work by helping to lower blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity. Injectable drugs, on the other hand, are administered via injections, such as insulin pens or syringes. These drugs are often used when oral medications are not effective enough in managing diabetes. Both types of drugs play a crucial role in controlling blood sugar levels and managing diabetes.</p></p>
