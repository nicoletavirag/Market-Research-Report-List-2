<p><p>A resorbable dental membrane is a barrier material used in guided tissue regeneration procedures in dentistry. It is designed to protect and stabilize the blood clot and bone graft material during the healing process, while also allowing for tissue regeneration to occur. These membranes gradually biodegrade and are absorbed by the body over time, eliminating the need for a second surgery to remove them.</p><p>The resorbable dental membrane market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years, with a projected CAGR of 9.20% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to factors such as the increasing prevalence of periodontal diseases, rising demand for dental procedures, advancements in technology leading to the development of innovative products, and an expanding geriatric population.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the resorbable dental membrane market include the introduction of novel materials with improved properties, such as enhanced biocompatibility and faster degradation rates. Cowhide membranes are known for their high tensile strength and durability, making them suitable for procedures that require long-term support. Both types of membranes are biocompatible and gradually resorb over time, eliminating the need for a second surgical procedure for removal.</p></p>
