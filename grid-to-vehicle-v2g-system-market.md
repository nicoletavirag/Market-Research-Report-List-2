<p><p>Grid-to-Vehicle (V2G) System market types include Centralized, Autonomous, and Micronet-based markets. In a Centralized market, power flow is managed by a central entity, such as a utility company, which coordinates charging and discharging of electric vehicles. In an Autonomous market, individual vehicles autonomously negotiate power transactions with the grid based on market conditions. The Micronet-based market involves a network of smaller entities, such as homes or businesses, that collectively participate in V2G transactions, offering flexibility and resilience to the overall grid system.</p></p>
