<p><p>Medical X-Ray Computer Tomography (CT) Scanners are categorized based on the number of detectors they have, which determines their imaging capabilities. Single-detector CT scanners use one detector to capture images, doubledetector CT scanners use two detectors, and multidetector CT scanners use multiple detectors to acquire images simultaneously. Multidetector CT scanners are more advanced as they can capture a larger volume of data in a shorter amount of time, improving image quality and diagnostic accuracy. This advancement in technology has led to a growing demand for multidetector CT scanners in the market.</p></p>
