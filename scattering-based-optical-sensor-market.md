<p><ul><li>Raman</li><li>Rayleigh</li><li>Brillouin</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Scattering-based optical sensors utilize different scattering phenomena to detect and analyze various substances. Raman scattering sensors use inelastic scattering to provide information about the chemical composition of a sample. Rayleigh scattering sensors detect changes in the frequency of light due to interactions with molecules in a sample. Brillouin scattering sensors measure the shift in frequency of light caused by acoustic waves in a material. Each type of scattering sensor offers unique capabilities for applications such as chemical analysis, temperature sensing, and material characterization.</p></p>
