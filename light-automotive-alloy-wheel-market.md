<p><p>Light automotive alloy wheels are primarily manufactured using three main techniques: casting, forging, and other methods. </p><p>Casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold and allowing it to cool and take shape. This process is cost-effective and is commonly used for mass production.</p><p>Forging involves shaping the wheel by applying pressure to the metal, resulting in a wheel that is strong and durable.</p><p>Other methods may include machining or spinning techniques to create the desired wheel design. Each method has its advantages and is chosen based on factors such as cost, production volume, and desired wheel characteristics.</p></p>
