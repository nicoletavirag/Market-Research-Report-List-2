<p><p>Masonry mortar is classified into four main types: Ordinary Cement Mortar, made of cement, sand, and water; Polymer Cement Mortar, containing a polymer additive for enhanced performance; Lime Mortar, which uses lime instead of cement for historical preservation; and Pozzolanic Mortar, incorporating pozzolanic materials like fly ash or silica fume for increased durability. Each type offers unique benefits and is used for specific applications in construction, serving different needs based on their properties and characteristics.</p></p>
