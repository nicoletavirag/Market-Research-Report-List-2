<p><p>Flush toilets are commonly categorized into different market types based on the type of mechanism used to control the flushing process. Some of the popular types include ball valve, self-control, barometric, and other variations. Ball valve flush toilets use a ball float mechanism to regulate water flow, while self-control toilets have a manual lever or button for users to control the flushing action. Barometric toilets utilize air pressure to flush effectively. Other market variations may include more advanced technology or design features for improved efficiency and convenience.</p></p>
