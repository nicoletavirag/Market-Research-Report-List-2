<p><p>Bone marrow transplant market includes two main types: autologous and allogeneic. Autologous bone marrow transplant involves using the patient's own stem cells, which are collected, frozen, and later infused back into the patient after high-dose chemotherapy. Allogeneic bone marrow transplant involves using stem cells from a donor, which may be a sibling or unrelated individual. Allogeneic transplants are often used for patients with certain types of cancer or blood disorders, as they provide healthy stem cells to replace diseased or damaged ones.</p></p>
