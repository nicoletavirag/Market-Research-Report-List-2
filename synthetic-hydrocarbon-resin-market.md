<p><p>Synthetic hydrocarbon resins are classified into three main types: C5, C9, and Others. C5 resins are made from aliphatic feedstocks and are known for their excellent tackifying properties. C9 resins are derived from aromatic feedstocks and are widely used in adhesives, coatings, and rubber industries. The "Others" category includes resins with different properties and compositions, such as hydrogenated hydrocarbon resins. Each type has specific characteristics that make them suitable for various applications in industries like adhesives, paints, and rubber compounding.</p></p>
